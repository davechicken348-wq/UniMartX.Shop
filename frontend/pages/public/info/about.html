The story

What used to happen on campus wasn't broken. It was abandoned.

If you are a student entrepreneur, your choices have always been the same: WhatsApp Status that disappears at midnight, Instagram posts buried under an algorithm you cannot control, or Telegram groups where orders get lost in the noise.

These channels are not designed for business. They are designed for attention. And when your business depends on being seen, the current setup worked against you — not for you.

I watched friends lose sales because a Status update expired. I watched sellers spend hours managing orders through DMs. I kept seeing the same pattern: smart students, good products, terrible infrastructure.

So I built something different.

What we believe

Principles, not features.

Four ideas that shaped every decision we made.

Permanence

Your business does not vanish after 24 hours. Your shop, your products, and your brand story stay live — available to anyone who searches for them, today or six months from now.

Ownership

You get your own branded space at unimartx.com/shop/your-brand. Not a profile, not a page. A real storefront with your name, your rules, your identity.

Access

Complex tools are expensive. UniMartX is free to start. No website builder, no prior experience, no upfront cost. Just a phone, a few products, and ten minutes.

Trust

Student commerce depends on reputation. We verify every seller and protect every transaction because we know word-of-mouth only works once.

Protected

The builder

Built by one person. Driven by purpose.

Every line of code, every design decision, and every feature shipped came from one person who refused to accept that campus trade had to be painful.

UniMartX was built by a single developer who got tired of watching students struggle with broken campus commerce — and decided to fix it.

AI helped fill some gaps, but the vision, the persistence, and the refusal to quit — that's all human. This isn't a side project. It's a mission.
